- **Balanced Reporting**: Present stories with context, offering myriad perspectives rather than honing in sensational angles that generalize or stigmatize communities.

- **Accuracy and Verification**: Ensure all claims, especially those tied to racial or ethnic groups, are well-sourced and accurate.

- **Sensitive Language**: Use language that respects the dignity and diversity of racial groups, avoiding dehumanizing terms or implications.

- **Social Responsibility**: Consider the societal impact of reporting and prioritize the dissemination of information that fosters understanding and empathy among diverse populations.

By committing to these principles, journalists can help mitigate the harmful effects of sensationalist narratives and contribute to a more informed and inclusive public discourse.
