   - **Description**: XSS attacks involve injecting malicious scripts into web pages that are then executed in the browsers of users visiting the page.
   - **Exploitation**: Attackers can steal session cookies, capture user credentials, or perform actions on behalf of the user.
   - **Defense Strategy**: Validate and sanitize all user inputs and implement Content Security Policy (CSP) headers to restrict the execution of unauthorized scripts.

3. **Privilege Escalation**:
   - **Description**: Privilege escalation attacks involve exploiting vulnerabilities or misconfigurations to gain unauthorized access to higher-level privileges.
   - **Exploitation**: Attackers could access sensitive areas of a network, execute commands, or obtain confidential data.
   - **Defense Strategy**: Regularly audit and apply the principle of least privilege to user accounts and systems. Keep systems patched to reduce exploitable paths.

4. **Phishing and Social Engineering**:
   - **Description**: Phishing attacks use deceptive methods to trick users into divulging sensitive information, like credentials or personal data.
   - **Exploitation**: These attacks can lead to account takeover, identity theft, or unauthorized transactions.

   - **Defense Strategy**: Use network monitoring tools to detect and respond to anomalous traffic patterns. Deploy DDoS mitigation solutions to filter malicious traffic.

   - **Defense Strategy**: Use encryption protocols like TLS to secure data-in-transit and employ strong authentication methods to verify identity.
